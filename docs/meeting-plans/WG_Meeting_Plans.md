## MEETING 1: KICKOFF (April 19–20, 2026 | 60 min | Zoom)

### Purpose
Orient the group, validate the evidence brief, and start generating candidate research questions.

### Pre-Meeting Preparation (Co-Leads)
- [ ] Review your WG description and evidence brief
- [ ] Review the literature review document for your WG
- [ ] Set up a shared workspace (Google Doc or shared folder) for async question development
- [ ] Confirm attendance with all members (aim for 100% at kickoff)
- [ ] Send calendar invite with Zoom link (Hope coordinates)

### Pre-Meeting Materials to Distribute (1 week before)
1. Working Group Description (your group's section)
2. AI-Generated Evidence Brief for your domain
3. Literature Review document for your WG
4. This meeting agenda
5. Link to shared Google Doc for question development

### Agenda

| Time | Item | Lead | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 0:00–0:08 | **Welcome & Introductions** | Co-Lead 1 | Each member: name, institution, 1 sentence on their angle on the topic |
| 0:08–0:12 | **Process Overview** | Co-Lead 2 | Walk through the 3-meeting arc, Delphi process, and deliverables. Set expectations: ~5–8 hours total over the next 4 weeks |
| 0:12–0:15 | **Role Assignments** | Co-Lead 1 | Decide: Who presents on conference day? Who drafts the 2-page summary? Who monitors the shared doc between meetings? |
| 0:15–0:30 | **Evidence Brief Discussion** | Co-Lead 2 facilitates | Structured discussion: (1) What rings true? (2) What's missing or wrong? (3) What surprised you? (4) Where is the evidence thinnest? |
| 0:30–0:50 | **Research Question Brainstorm** | Both co-leads | Goal: Generate 15–25 raw candidate questions. Use the prompt: "What are the most important unanswered research questions in [your domain] that could be addressed in the next 10 years?" Capture ALL ideas — no filtering yet. |
| 0:50–0:55 | **Async Work Plan** | Co-Lead 1 | Assign: Each member adds 2–3 additional questions to the shared doc by April 25. Co-leads will organize/deduplicate before Round 1 survey. |
| 0:55–1:00 | **Questions & Wrap-Up** | Both | Confirm next meeting date (May 2–3). Remind about async deadlines. |

3. **Raw question list** — All brainstormed questions, unedited, attributed to whoever raised them
4. **Role assignments** — Who is doing what
5. **Communication preferences** — Email thread, Slack, shared doc, etc.

### Facilitation Tips
- Keep introductions tight (30 sec each). You have 8–10 people; this eats time fast.
- For the brainstorm, consider a 5-min silent writing period first (each person types 3 questions into the shared doc), then discuss. This prevents anchoring.
- Don't evaluate questions yet. "That's a good one" is fine; "I don't think that's testable" should wait for the async refinement phase.
- If someone dominates, redirect: "Let's hear from someone who hasn't spoken yet."

## MEETING 2: MID-POINT REVIEW (May 2–3, 2026 | 45 min | Zoom)

### Purpose
Review Delphi Round 1 results, discuss borderline questions, revise and finalize Round 2 survey.

### Pre-Meeting Preparation (Co-Leads)
- [ ] Review Round 1 survey results (planning committee will compile and format)
- [ ] Identify: Which questions hit ≥80% include? Which hit ≤20%? Which are in the gray zone?
- [ ] Flag questions with substantive written comments for discussion
- [ ] Prepare a summary slide or document showing results (planning committee can help)

### Pre-Meeting Materials to Distribute (2–3 days before)
1. Round 1 results summary (% include / modify / exclude for each question)
2. Anonymized written comments on each question
3. Any new questions suggested in the open-ended section
4. This meeting agenda

### Agenda

| Time | Item | Lead | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| 0:00–0:03 | **Welcome & Recap** | Co-Lead 1 | Brief recap of where we are in the process |
| 0:03–0:10 | **Round 1 Overview** | Co-Lead 2 | Present summary: How many questions? Response rate? How many hit consensus (≥80%)? How many excluded (≤20%)? How many in the gray zone? |
| 0:10–0:15 | **Celebrate Consensus** | Co-Lead 1 | Quick walk-through of confirmed questions (≥80%). Any concerns? If not, these are locked. |
| 0:15–0:35 | **Gray Zone Discussion** | Both | This is the core of the meeting. For each borderline question (21–79%): (1) Read the written comments. (2) Discuss: Is the issue wording, scope, or genuine disagreement? (3) Decide: Revise and advance to Round 2, merge with another question, or drop. |
| 0:35–0:40 | **New Question Review** | Co-Lead 2 | Were any new questions suggested in Round 1? Worth adding to Round 2? |
| 0:40–0:43 | **Round 2 Survey Prep** | Co-Lead 1 | Confirm final question list for Round 2. Planning committee will build and distribute within 24 hours. |
| 0:43–0:45 | **Wrap-Up** | Both | Round 2 window: May 3–9 (5 days). Confirm Meeting 3 date (May 10–11). |

### Facilitation Tips
- Spend almost no time on confirmed or excluded questions. The gray zone is the whole meeting.
- For each gray-zone question, ask: "Is this a wording problem or a substance problem?" Wording problems get revised; substance disagreements may need to stay for Round 2 to resolve.
- If discussion stalls on one question, timebox it (3 min max) and move on. You can revisit if time allows.
- Remind the group that Round 2 shows them the voting percentages — this often resolves borderline items without further discussion.

## CROSS-CUTTING: DELPHI SURVEY INSTRUMENTS

### Round 1 Survey Template

**Header:**
> SAEM 2026 AI Consensus Conference — Delphi Round 1
> Working Group [#]: [Name]
> 
> Please rate each candidate research question below. Your responses are anonymous to other group members. The co-leads and planning committee will see aggregate results only.
> 
> For each question, select one:
> - **Include** — This question should be part of the 10-year research agenda
> - **Include with modifications** — Important topic, but the question needs rewording (please suggest changes)
> - **Exclude** — This question should not be prioritized in the research agenda
> 
> Rating scale (1–9): How important is this research question for the field?
> 1 = Not important | 5 = Moderately important | 9 = Critically important

**Per question:**
- Question text
- Include / Include with modifications / Exclude (radio)
- Importance rating (1–9 Likert scale)
- Comments / suggested modifications (open text)

**End of survey:**
- "Are there any important research questions missing from this list? Please suggest up to 3 additional questions." (open text)
- "Any other comments about the scope or direction of this working group?" (open text)

### Round 2 Survey Template

**Header:**
> SAEM 2026 AI Consensus Conference — Delphi Round 2
> Working Group [#]: [Name]
> 
> Below are the revised research questions for your working group. For each question, you can see how the group voted in Round 1. Questions that reached ≥80% consensus in Round 1 are already confirmed and not included here.
> 
> Please rate each remaining question. This is the final round.

**Per question:**
- Question text (revised if applicable; note: "Revised based on Round 1 feedback")
- Round 1 result: ___% Include | ___% Modify | ___% Exclude
- Your Round 2 vote: Include / Exclude (binary — no "modify" option in Round 2)
- Importance rating (1–9 Likert scale)
- Final comments (open text)

**End of survey:**
- "Looking at the full list (confirmed + remaining), are there any critical gaps?" (open text)
- "Any final thoughts before the conference?" (open text)

### Consensus Thresholds (Applied After Each Round)
| Result | Action |
|--------|--------|
| ≥80% include | **Confirmed.** Goes on the final list. |
| 21–79% include | **Discuss.** Co-leads decide; note but don't include if no resolution. |
| ≤20% include | **Removed.** |

Track % agreement at both rounds for every question — this data is required for the proceedings manuscript.

## AI-ENHANCED METHODOLOGY

This conference uses a novel, multi-layered approach to consensus building. Full details are in `docs/methodology/`. Key additions beyond the standard Delphi:

### Pairwise Comparison Ranking
Alongside each Delphi round, participants complete a quick pairwise ranking exercise through the conference platform that produces a continuous priority ranking using Bradley-Terry scoring. See `docs/methodology/Pairwise_Comparison_Setup_Guide.md`.

**Co-lead action:** Direct participants to the pairwise section of the conference platform alongside the Delphi survey. Review pairwise rankings at Meeting 2 alongside Delphi results.

### AI-Augmented Inter-Round Synthesis
Between Delphi rounds, AI synthesis tools process free-text comments to cluster themes, suggest question revisions, and detect cross-WG overlap. Co-leads receive a structured report and accept/reject/modify each AI suggestion.

**Co-lead action:** Review the AI synthesis report before Meeting 2. All decisions documented.

See `docs/methodology/AI_Synthesis_Prompts.md` for the full prompt library.

### Conference-Day Electronic Data Collection
Audience voting captured electronically through the conference platform with real-time SSE updates, pre/post deliberation comparison, and cross-WG prioritization. Offline backup protocols in place.

See `docs/methodology/Conference_Day_Data_Collection.md`.

### Cross-WG AI Analysis
After Delphi completes, AI-assisted analysis identifies overlaps, gaps, and tensions across all 5 WGs. Results inform the conference-day synthesis presentation.

See `docs/methodology/Cross_WG_Analysis_Pipeline.md`.
