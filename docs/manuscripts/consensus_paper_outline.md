## ABSTRACT (~300 words)

**Objectives:** The Society for Academic Emergency Medicine (SAEM) 2026 Consensus Conference convened to (1) develop a 10-year research agenda for artificial intelligence (AI) in emergency medicine across five domains — clinical practice, infrastructure and data, education, human-AI interaction, and ethics — and (2) pilot a novel AI-enhanced, multi-method consensus process integrating modified Delphi surveys, pairwise comparison ranking, and conference-day live voting.

**Methods:** Pre-conference, five working groups (N=[X] domain experts) conducted structured literature reviews and generated candidate research questions. A two-round modified Delphi process with predefined consensus thresholds (>=80% include for confirmation) was conducted concurrently with a pairwise comparison survey. Between rounds, an AI synthesis pipeline assisted with qualitative data analysis under human-in-the-loop oversight. On conference day, [N] attendees participated in working group presentations, breakout deliberations, and cross-working-group prioritization voting.

**Results:** [X] candidate research questions entered the Delphi process across five working groups. After two rounds, [X] questions achieved consensus (>=80% include), [X] reached near-consensus (60-79%), and [X] were removed. Conference-day prioritization identified [the top priorities]. Three-way concordance between Delphi importance, pairwise ranking, and conference-day allocation was [moderate/strong] (Kendall's W = [X]). The resulting research agenda spans [X] thematic areas with [describe key patterns].

**Conclusions:** This consensus conference produced a comprehensive, empirically prioritized 10-year research agenda for AI in emergency medicine. The multi-method approach provided convergent evidence for the highest-priority questions while the AI-enhanced synthesis pipeline demonstrated [feasibility/efficiency gains] under transparent human oversight. The research agenda addresses the full translational spectrum from technology development to ethical governance and calls for coordinated investment across the [key pillars identified].

## INTRODUCTION (~1,000 words)

### The AI Moment in Emergency Medicine
- Rapid expansion of AI applications across healthcare
- Emergency medicine as a uniquely high-stakes, time-pressured, data-rich environment
- Current state: growing number of validated models, few deployed in clinical practice
- Gap between AI capability and clinical implementation

### The Need for a Coordinated Research Agenda
- Proliferation of isolated studies without a unifying framework
- Risk of fragmented investment and duplicated effort
- Lessons from precision medicine consensus (Strehlow et al. 2024) — value of structured agenda-setting
- Prior SAEM consensus conferences and their impact on the field

### Conference Scope and Framework
- Four-pillar framework: Technology, Training, Self, and Society
- Rationale for this framing — AI's effects are not purely technical
- Five working groups spanning the full translational pathway
- Conference aims:
  1. Develop a prioritized 10-year research agenda for AI in EM
  2. Identify critical knowledge gaps across five domains
  3. Establish cross-domain connections and productive tensions
  4. Provide actionable guidance for funders, institutions, and investigators

### Innovation in Methodology
- Brief note on the AI-enhanced multi-method approach (detailed in companion methodology paper)
- How the process itself reflects the conference theme — using AI to study AI
